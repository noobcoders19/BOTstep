Refilling Process

Follow these simple steps to refill your water container.

1. Place Container in Volume Bay

Carefully position your container under the dispenser for filling.

The process involves carefully placing the container into the water, ensuring it is fully submerged without splashing, which causes the water level to rise. The change in water level represents the displaced volume, which can vary depending on whether the water is measured as free-flowing (bulk volume), confined (container volume), or calibrated in a measuring device. After observing the displacement, the water can be refilled to the desired level, taking into account the type of water volume being measured, whether for precise scientific measurement or general approximation.

2. Select Water Type

Choose the water type â€” mineral, purified, or alkaline.

When using the filling bay, it is important to first select the water type by carefully choosing the correct option on the control panel, valve, or dispenser. This step ensures that the water being dispensed whether it is potable for drinking, deionized for laboratory or industrial use, or process water for equipment and cleaning is appropriate for the intended purpose. Once the water type is selected, the system activates the corresponding water line, allowing the container or tank to be filled safely and efficiently while preventing contamination or damage from using the wrong type of water

3. Start Water Dispensing

Watch as your container fills automatically with pure water.

After selecting the appropriate water type in the filling bay, you can start water dispensing by activating the pump or opening the valve, allowing the chosen water to flow into the container or tank safely and efficiently while monitoring the process to prevent overflow or contamination

4. Payment Processing

Complete your transaction easily via cash or digital payment.

To complete the transaction at the filling bay, the customer selects the desired water type, reviews the cost, completes the payment using cash, card, or mobile method, and upon confirmation, the system allows the water dispensing to begin safely and efficiently.

5. Take Your Refilled Water

Collect your container and ensure the cap is securely sealed.

After completing the selection of the water type and the payment process at the filling bay, customers take the refilled water in their containers or tanks, ensuring that it is properly sealed or capped before transport to maintain cleanliness and prevent spillage
